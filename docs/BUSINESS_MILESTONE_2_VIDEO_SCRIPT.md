# Business Milestone 2 Animated Video Script

## 操作

推荐形式：

- 用 PowerPoint、Google Slides 或 Canva 录制
- 做成 7 页左右的 investor-style research video
- 每页尽量只放 1 个核心结论
- 用大标题 + 1 个关键数字 + 1 个图表/图标
- 动画建议继续只用 `Fade`、`Appear`、`Zoom`

整体风格建议：

- 这次不是讲“为什么这个产品有意义”
- 而是讲“为什么这个市场值得做，而且这个产品有赚钱机会”
- 语气更像 investor update，而不是面向普通用户的宣传片
- 不要塞太多技术细节，重点讲市场、空白、商业模式、ROI

建议时长：

- 90 到 120 秒

## Script

This market is already huge, but the value is still flowing mostly to the insurer side.

According to NAIC market share data, private passenger auto insurance in the U.S. accounted for about 344 billion dollars in direct written premiums, or roughly 35 percent of all written premiums. That means the auto claims workflow is not a niche problem.

California alone is already a strong starting wedge. As of January 1, 2025, California had nearly 36 million registered vehicles. In the NAIC auto insurance database report, California’s combined average premium was 1,417 dollars and 94 cents per vehicle in 2023.

The second key point is that the industry already pays for claims AI. CCC says it connects more than 35,000 businesses and processes 100 billion dollars in transactions annually. Snapsheet says it works with more than 140 customers. Tractable reports an 8-day reduction in cycle time and a 50 percent reduction in estimate writing time. Hi Marley case studies show fewer phone calls and faster total-loss settlement.

So the question is not whether claims software creates value. It clearly does.

The real gap is who benefits. Most current products are built for carriers, adjusters, and insurer operations. ClaimMate is built for the policyholder. We want to give the driver the same information advantage that enterprise tools already give the insurance company.

Our pricing reflects the reality of the problem. Claims are low-frequency events, so a monthly subscription is a weak fit. A per-case unlock is much easier to understand. The model is simple: free entry for basic help, then a 4.99 dollar paid unlock for deeper claim support, deadline tracking, dispute guidance, and exports.

Even at California-only scale, low conversion can still matter. As an illustrative example, if just 0.5 percent of California’s registered vehicles translated into one paid case unlock in a year, that would be about 180 thousand paid cases, or roughly 0.9 million dollars in gross revenue. At 1 percent, that becomes about 1.8 million dollars. This is not a forecast, but it shows why a low-ticket model can still be meaningful at state scale.

Our go-to-market starts at the moment of need: policy confusion, accident intake, claim delay, and claim denial. That creates high-intent entry points, and the structured report and shared claim context can also create referral loops inside the claims workflow itself.

Milestone 2 matters because it reduces business risk, not just technical risk. We now have a working foundation for policy Q&A, structured accident context, and AI-assisted claim support. That moves ClaimMate closer to a real product with a believable path to monetization.
